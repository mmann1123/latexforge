Subject: Introducing LaTeX Forge -- A Free Collaborative LaTeX Editor for Our Department

Hi everyone,

I wanted to share a tool I've been building that I think many of you will find useful: LaTeX Forge (https://latexforge.web.app).

What is it?

LaTeX Forge is a lightweight, web-based LaTeX editor -- think of it as a simpler alternative to Overleaf. You write LaTeX in your browser, hit compile, and get a PDF. No software to install, no subscription required.

Why I built it

As many of you know, Overleaf's free tier has become increasingly limited -- restricted collaborators, compile timeouts, and features locked behind a paywall. I wanted something our department could use freely for teaching, co-authoring papers, and student projects without worrying about subscription costs or seat limits.

LaTeX Forge is hosted on our own infrastructure, so your documents stay under our control, and there are no artificial restrictions on collaboration or compile time.

Key features

- Real-time collaboration -- Multiple people can edit the same document simultaneously with live cursors and edits
- Instant PDF preview -- Full pdflatex and BibTeX compilation, right in the browser
- Multi-file projects -- Organize your work with folders, images, .bib files, and multiple .tex files
- Inline comments -- Leave line-level comments for your co-authors, similar to Google Docs or a code review
- Smart editor -- Syntax highlighting, LaTeX autocompletion, spell checking, and a document outline for navigation
- Secure -- Google authentication with access limited to .edu and .org accounts, per-project permissions (owner/editor/viewer)

How to get started

1. Go to https://latexforge.web.app
2. Sign in with your .edu or .org Google account - If your org doesn't use Google, let me know and I can set up an alternative authentication method
3. Create a new project and start writing

To collaborate, use the Share button inside any project to invite co-authors by email.

A note on beta status

LaTeX Forge is currently in beta. The core functionality -- editing, compiling, collaboration, and file management -- is stable and safe to use for your work. Your documents are saved in real time to cloud storage, so you won't lose anything. That said, you may encounter rough edges in the interface or occasional quirks. If you run into any issues or have feature requests, please let me know directly -- your feedback will help me improve the tool.

I'd encourage you to give it a try on your next paper, problem set, or class notes. I'm happy to answer any questions or walk through the tool with anyone interested.
